About the Conference - ICE2CPT 2026

The 2026 IEEE 2nd International Conference on Electrical, Electronics and Computer Science with Advanced Power Technologies – A Future Trend (ICE2CPT 2026) will be hosted by CORE University from 19th to 21st November 2026 in hybrid mode. This premier event is being held in association with IEEE and the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society. As a flagship international conference of the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society, ICE2CPT 2026 is poised to serve as a global platform for researchers, academicians, and industry professionals to exchange cutting-edge developments in electrical engineering, electronics, computer science, and advanced power technologies. ICE2CPT 2026 aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration by highlighting emerging trends and innovations in areas such as smart grids, artificial intelligence, Internet of Things (IoT) applications, signal processing, advanced control systems, and sustainable energy technologies. The conference will facilitate knowledge sharing and thought leadership across academia and industry, addressing critical challenges and opportunities in green industrial electronics and digital transformation. By encouraging dynamic discussions and strategic networking, ICE2CPT 2026 aspires to shape the future landscape of intelligent and sustainable energy and communication systems.

Publication Opportunity

Accepted and presented papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore, subject to meeting IEEE Xplore’s scope and quality requirements. All submissions must be written in English. All papers must be original unpublished work.

Special Sessions

The conference will include special sessions on highly specialized topic areas, reporting technical trends and breakthroughs within the scope of the conference. Special Sessions are organized at the initiative of one or more individuals, who must adhere to specific procedures published here.

Paper Submission Guidelines

All papers must be submitted electronically in PDF format by the deadline. The maximum length for full papers is 6 pages, including all text, tables, figures, and references. All authors are encouraged to use as much of the allowed space to effectively explain their work. The conference proceedings will be submitted to the IEEE Xplore Digital Library®. Standard manuscript templates for conference proceedings in LaTex and Word are present at Standard IEEE Templates.
