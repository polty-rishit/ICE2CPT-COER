Message from the General Chair

Dear Esteemed Colleagues,

It is my great honor and pleasure to welcome you, on behalf of the Organizing Committee, to the 2026 IEEE 2nd International Conference on Electrical, Electronics and Computer Science with Advanced Power Technologies – A Future Trend (ICE2CPT 2026). The conference will be held from November 19–21, 2026, in hybrid mode, organized by CORE University.

ICE2CPT 2026 is technically sponsored by IEEE and the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society, and serves as a premier platform for academicians, researchers, industry professionals, and students from across the globe to exchange knowledge, share innovative ideas, and discuss recent advancements in electrical engineering, electronics, computer science, and advanced power technologies.

The proceedings of the first edition of ICE2CPT have been successfully published in IEEE Xplore, reflecting the high quality and global impact of the conference. The proceedings can be accessed here. This achievement reinforces ICE2CPT as a credible and impactful platform within the international research community.

Building on this strong foundation, the second edition will emphasize emerging trends in sustainable energy systems, intelligent technologies, smart communication, automation, and advanced control systems, addressing key challenges and opportunities shaping the future of engineering and technology.

The technical program will feature keynote addresses by distinguished experts, high-quality paper presentations, tutorials, workshops, and panel discussions, designed to foster innovation, collaboration, and knowledge dissemination. ICE2CPT 2026 aims to create a dynamic environment that encourages networking and promotes impactful research outcomes.

I warmly invite you to actively participate in this prestigious conference and contribute your expertise toward advancing science, engineering, and technology. We look forward to welcoming you to ICE2CPT 2026 for an intellectually enriching and professionally rewarding experience.
